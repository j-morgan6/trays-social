# Community Guidelines

Trays is a place for home cooks to share recipes, swap notes, and learn from each other. These guidelines are how we keep it that way. They're a plain-language companion to the formal rules in our [Terms of Service](/terms) §5.

## What we love to see

- **Real recipes you've actually made** — with ingredients, steps, and the kind of details a cook trying it for the first time would need
- **Honest comments** — "I tried this with chicken instead of tofu and it worked" is gold; so is "the bake time was too long for my oven"
- **Generous credit** — if a recipe came from someone else (a cookbook, a relative, another cook on Trays), say so in the caption
- **Helpful corrections** — "FYI, this needs an egg or it won't bind" is welcome; condescension isn't

## What's not allowed

These categories will get content removed and may get accounts suspended or terminated. We err on the side of removal when there's doubt.

### Harassment, hate, or threats

- Personal attacks, insults, or pile-ons against another cook
- Slurs or content targeting people based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexuality, disability, or any other identity
- Threats of violence, doxxing (sharing someone's personal info without consent), or coordinated harassment campaigns

### Sexual content, graphic violence, illegal content

- Sexual content, nudity, or sexually suggestive material
- Graphic violence, gore, or content depicting cruelty (including animal cruelty in cooking contexts)
- Content promoting or instructing on illegal activity — including illegal drugs, illegal foraging, or recipes designed to harm

### Spam, scams, and manipulation

- Repeated identical or near-identical posts
- Commercial spam, link farming, or off-platform redirects unrelated to a recipe
- Engagement manipulation — buying followers, vote brigading, sock-puppet accounts, or coordinated inauthentic behavior
- Bot or automated accounts unless we've explicitly authorized them

### Impersonation and misinformation

- Pretending to be another person, business, or public figure
- Misleading recipes that pose a safety risk (e.g., unsafe canning practices, raw consumption of foods that require cooking) presented as safe
- Health or dietary claims framed as medical advice

### Content that isn't yours

- Posting someone else's photo or recipe without credit or permission
- Copyright infringement of any kind — see our [DMCA process](/terms) in §7 of the Terms

### Attacks on the platform

- Attempting to break, exploit, or disrupt Trays' service
- Scraping, automated extraction, or bulk download of other cooks' content

## Cooking-specific judgment calls

Food is personal and Trays is a global community. A few things we lean into:

- **Diet styles are welcome side-by-side.** Vegan posts on omnivore recipes (or vice versa) are fine; "your diet is wrong" comments are not.
- **Cultural recipes deserve attribution.** A dish has a history. Naming where it comes from is generous; claiming you invented something you didn't is not.
- **Allergies and safety belong in the recipe.** If your recipe contains common allergens (nuts, dairy, eggs, shellfish, gluten), say so. If a step is genuinely dangerous if done wrong (raw meat, hot oil, pressure canning), flag it.

## How moderation works

You have three tools in the app:

- **Report a post, comment, or user** — opens a short form; a moderator reviews. Reports are confidential to the moderator.
- **Block a user** — hides their posts, comments, and notifications from you everywhere in Trays. They are not notified.
- **Delete your own content or your whole account** — Settings → Delete account; this is irreversible and clears your data per the [Privacy Policy](/privacy).

When a moderator removes content or suspends an account, the user is told the broad reason (e.g., "harassment") but not which report triggered the action. We do this so reporting is safe.

## Appeals

If your content is removed or your account is suspended and you believe it was a mistake, email `support@trays.app` within **14 days** of the action. We acknowledge appeals within 7 days and decide within 30 days.
